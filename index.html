About

I am a Physics PhD student at the University of Iowa, working in the Quantum Light Control Lab under Prof. Ravitej Uppu. My research focuses on quantum optics, nanophotonics, wavefront shaping, and spin-photon interfaces for chip-integrated photonic quantum technologies.

I'm particularly interested in investigating the coherence properties of quantum emitters in heteroepitaxial light-matter interfaces for applications in photonic quantum communication and computation. My work involves nanofabrication, computational electrodynamics simulations, and cryogenic optical spectroscopy.

Prior to my PhD, I completed my Bachelor of Science in Physics (Honors) and Mathematics at the University of Iowa, graduating with Highest Distinction. My undergraduate research experience included projects on wavefront shaping through multimode optical fibers and spatial division multiplexing using orbital angular momentum states.

Research Interests

My research spans several interconnected areas in quantum photonics and nanoscale optics, with applications in quantum information science.

Multimode Quantum Optics

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit. Multimode quantum optics involves studying the quantum nature of light and its interaction with matter at the quantum level. My research focuses on single-photon sources, quantum interference, and photon-photon interactions that are essential for quantum information processing.

This field provides the theoretical foundation for many quantum technologies including quantum computing, quantum cryptography, and quantum sensing.

Nanophotonics

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit. Nanophotonics explores how light behaves and interacts with materials at the nanoscale. My work involves designing and characterizing nanophotonic structures such as photonic crystal cavities, waveguides, and metamaterials that can enhance light-matter interactions.

These engineered structures can be used to control the emission, propagation, and detection of quantum light with unprecedented precision.

Hybrid Spin-photon Interfaces

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit. Spin-photon interfaces connect the world of quantum optics with solid-state quantum information processing. My work explores how electron spins in semiconductor quantum dots and other solid-state systems can be efficiently coupled to photons.

These interfaces form the building blocks for quantum repeaters, quantum memory, and ultimately a quantum internet that can connect quantum computers across long distances.

Research Experience

Graduate Research Assistant | May 2023 - Present

As a graduate researcher in Prof. Ravitej Uppu's lab, I focus on:

  • Researching the coherence properties of quantum emitters in heteroepitaxial light-matter interfaces for applications in photonic quantum communication and computation
  • Developing nanofabrication protocols for III-V semiconductor micropillars, nanobeams and photonic crystal waveguides using UV photolithography and electron-beam lithography
  • Generating 3D computational electrodynamics simulations of quantum emitters in homogeneous and heterogeneous nanobeam waveguides
  • Constructing cryogenic-compatible optical setups for performing resonance fluorescence spectroscopy at telecom-band wavelengths to investigate the coherence properties of Er³⁺ ions doped in bulk GaAs
